Our story

From a 2 kg sample roaster in a Belmont basement to ten years of small-batch.

How Northwind started

In 2016, Sam Reyes bought a 2 kg sample roaster off Craigslist and put it in the basement of a rented house on SE Belmont. The idea was simple: roast small batches of beans they sourced themselves on a trip through Ethiopia, and sell them to a handful of cafés in Portland.

The first batch sold out in a week. The second batch sold out in three days. By the end of 2017 they had moved into a real space on Belmont — six blocks from where the basement was — and added a second roaster.

Ten years later we still run only two roasters. We could have grown faster. We didn't, because the thing we wanted to do well — roast coffee the week it ships, from beans we bought from the farm directly — doesn't scale through automation. It scales through staying small and roasting often.

What we mean by direct-trade

Every lot we sell, we paid the farm directly for. There is no broker. Each year, one of us flies to one of the origins on our list and meets with farms we've been buying from and farms we'd like to try.

We pay above the C-market price by a margin we publish on each bag. In 2025 the average margin was 71% above C-market.

What we don't do

We don't do flavored coffee, nitro cold brew, or pumpkin anything. We don't have a punch card. We don't do bottomless refills. If you want any of those things, we are genuinely happy to recommend a place a few blocks away that does.
